## 1. SAM.gov Registration & Government Contracts

My first suggestion is that MX Genius should register on SAM.gov and begin positioning itself for federal contracting — specifically through the DoD's SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research) program.

Here's why this matters: what we've built checks nearly every box that the Department of Defense is actively looking for right now. Edge inference, AI-driven predictive maintenance, autonomous on-board processing, offline-capable speech — these aren't nice-to-haves for them, they're named priorities. The Air Force's innovation arm, AFWERX, runs both Open Topic and Specific Topic solicitations throughout the year specifically targeting these capabilities.

**What we bring to the table:**
- Bespoke edge AI that runs entirely on-device — no cloud dependency
- Avionics maintenance intelligence built on real-world mechanic expertise
- A fully offline-capable, air-gapped architecture with on-board speech processing
- A team that combines deep development capability with a large network of certified avionics mechanics

That last point is the real differentiator. Most tech companies that bid on these contracts don't have the domain expertise. Most maintenance shops don't have the tech. We have both.

**The AFWERX SBIR program operates in phases:**

| Phase | Purpose | Award |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Phase I | Technical feasibility study | Up to $75K |
| Phase II | Prototype development | Up to $2M |
| Direct to Phase II | For solutions already prototype-ready | Up to $1.25M |
| STRATFI/TACFI | Scaling and transition funding | $375K – $15M |

Given that MxGenius is already a working product — not a concept — we could potentially qualify for a Direct to Phase II submission, which starts at up to $1.25 million.

**What it takes:** SAM.gov registration is free and takes about 10–15 business days. We'd need the MX Genius Corporation's TIN, banking info, and NAICS codes. Once registered, we can begin responding to solicitations immediately. I'd recommend we start monitoring the DSIP portal (dodsbirsttr.mil) and the AFWERX Open Topic cycle for relevant postings.

## 2. R&D Tax Credits — Free Money (We Already Qualify)

This one is straightforward, and I want to make sure it's on your radar because it's essentially money left on the table if we don't claim it.

Under IRC Section 41, businesses that develop bespoke software — custom code built to solve technical problems through experimentation — are eligible for the federal Research & Development tax credit. MxGenius qualifies cleanly under the IRS four-part test:

1. **Permitted Purpose** — We developed a new product (AI-driven avionics maintenance platform)
2. **Technological in Nature** — The work relies on computer science, machine learning, and software engineering
3. **Technical Uncertainty** — There was genuine uncertainty in how to implement edge inference, offline AI, and real-time avionics data integration
4. **Process of Experimentation** — We iterated through prototypes, model training, architecture decisions, and systematic testing

**What changed recently:** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed July 2025, restored immediate expensing of domestic R&E costs under the new Section 174A. This means the company can fully deduct qualifying research expenses in the year they're incurred — no more five-year amortization. For a company in its first year, that's a significant cash flow advantage.

**Qualified Research Expenses (QREs) include:**
- Developer wages (if 80%+ of time is on qualified research, the full salary qualifies)
- Contract research payments (65% of third-party payments are eligible)
- Cloud computing and infrastructure costs used in the research
- Supplies and tangible property consumed in the process

**Small business bonus:** Qualifying small businesses with less than $31M in average annual gross receipts may also be able to retroactively amend returns for 2022–2024 under the transition rules.

I'd recommend the corporation consult with a CPA familiar with Section 41 claims as part of its initial tax strategy. This isn't speculative — it's established law, and the credit exists specifically for what we've been doing.

## 3. Federal Grants — High Demand, Perfect Fit

Forming the MX Genius Corporation doesn't just open the door to contracts — it also makes us eligible for non-dilutive grant funding that wasn't available to us before.

**SBIR/STTR Grants (DoD, NASA, FAA)**
The same SBIR program mentioned above isn't just contracts — the Phase I awards are essentially grants for feasibility studies. NASA and the FAA also run their own SBIR/STTR programs with topics directly relevant to what we do:
- NASA frequently solicits for aerospace AI, predictive maintenance, and autonomous systems
- The FAA issues research grants and cooperative agreements for projects advancing aviation safety and technology
- All of these are searchable through SBIR.gov and Grants.gov

**FAA Aviation Research Grants**
The FAA periodically releases Broad Agency Announcements (BAAs) for research in flight systems, safety technologies, and AI/ML applications. These aren't workforce development grants — they're specifically for technology R&D.

**APEX Accelerators (formerly PTACs)**
These are free government-funded organizations that exist specifically to help small businesses navigate federal procurement and grants. They'll help us identify the right agencies, review proposals, and understand compliance requirements. There's no reason not to use them.

**Key point:** All of these funding sources are *non-dilutive* — they don't take equity. We get the capital to develop and demonstrate the technology, and we keep full ownership of what we build. Combined with the R&D tax credit, the financial runway extends significantly without giving up any of the 20/20 ownership structure you're putting together.
